For estimators, quantity surveyors and building consultants

You already tell them to charge for it

Your builders know they should be paid for preliminary work. Most of them agree with you in the meeting and go back to quoting for free by Friday. Not because they disagree. Because nobody gave them the process.

The gap between the advice and the change

If you advise residential builders, you have had this conversation more times than you can count. The estimating time is unpaid. The win rate is 20%. Four out of five detailed quotes are done for nothing, and the builder is wearing it.

You tell them to charge. They agree. Then nothing changes.

It is worth being precise about why, because it is not stubbornness.

They cannot say the number

A builder who can price a job to the dollar still cannot say "that costs $2,500" without their voice changing. It is not a pricing problem. It is a process problem, which is fixable.

A fee with nothing around it

Charging for a quote sounds like charging for a sales document. Without a named package, deliverables the client keeps, and a credit against the build, homeowners decline and the builder concludes it does not work.

So the advice is correct and it does not land. Not because the builder rejected it, but because "start charging" is a decision and what they needed was a process.

What we install

PreBuild is the front-end process that sits under the advice you already give. It is done for you, not another system the builder has to learn and run.

  1. Every enquiry answered immediatelyIncluding at 9pm on a Sunday, which is when a large share of building enquiries actually arrive. The builder does not have to be awake.
  2. Consistent qualificationBudget, timeline, project type and site, asked the same way every time and recorded rather than remembered. The estimating hours stop going to people whose budget was never close.
  3. The preliminary conversation, earnedThe fee is introduced after value has been shown, attached to a named package with deliverables the homeowner keeps, credited against the build. That is the structure that makes the answer yes.
  4. Follow-up that survives a busy weekPaid quotes that go quiet are the expensive ones. The sequence runs whether or not anyone remembers.
